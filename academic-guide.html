Academic Guide

Your complete academic guide at NAUB

Grading System

NAUB uses a 5-point grading system to evaluate student performance:

Grade Percentage Points Description
A 70 - 100% 5 Excellent
B 60 - 69% 4 Very Good
C 50 - 59% 3 Good
D 45 - 49% 2 Pass
F 0 - 44% 0 Fail

Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA)

Your CGPA is calculated by multiplying each course grade point by the course credit units, summing all products, and dividing by the total credit units.

  • First Class: 4.50 - 5.00
  • Second Class Upper: 3.50 - 4.49
  • Second Class Lower: 2.50 - 3.49
  • Third Class: 1.50 - 2.49
  • Pass: 1.00 - 1.49
  • Fail: Below 1.00

Examination Rules

  • Arrive at the examination venue at least 30 minutes early
  • Bring your student ID card
  • No electronic devices allowed
  • No communication during examination
  • Leave the venue only after submission

Academic Support

Academic Advisors

Each student is assigned an academic advisor who provides guidance on course selection and academic progress.

Office Hours

Lecturers hold office hours each week. Check with your department for specific times.

Library Services

  • Reference materials
  • Online databases
  • Inter-library loan
  • Research assistance

Academic Probation

Students with CGPA below 1.00 will be placed on academic probation and required to improve their performance.
